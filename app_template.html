🛥️ Lake Winnipeg Field Course — MV NAMAO Data Explorer

Limnology & Oceanography Field Course · rosette water chemistry · an interactive companion to Ocean Data View (ODV)

Welcome aboard

This is a self-contained explorer for the water-chemistry data your class collected from Lake Winnipeg aboard the MV NAMAO in June 2025, using rosette/Niskin casts at a series of stations. It runs entirely in your browser — no internet needed — so you can keep it on a USB stick or in Google Drive and open it anywhere.

Think of it as a friendly, opinionated sibling to Ocean Data View (ODV). The tabs mirror the core things ODV does, so you can build intuition here and then reproduce — and go beyond — these plots in ODV yourself.

Two datasets

This explorer pulls together the two ways you sampled the water column:

  • Bottle chemistry — discrete water samples from the rosette/Niskin bottles, analysed in the lab for nutrients, isotopes, carbonate system, major ions and more. A handful of depths per station, but lots of variables. Powers Bottle Profiles, Property–Property, Section and the Data Table.
  • RBR CTD casts — the sensor package logging ~2 readings per second as it descends, giving high-resolution profiles of temperature, salinity, oxygen, chlorophyll, CDOM, turbidity and PAR. Powers the CTD Casts tab.
  • Live YSI sonde — real-time Bluetooth + GPS readings you stream and log on the Live tab. Anything you log becomes a third Live sonde dataset you can explore in Property–Property, Section and the Data Table.

The Station Map shows where both were collected, over real satellite imagery.

What's in each tab

🗺️ Station Map

Where every sample was taken, on a pre-fetched satellite basemap (works offline) drawn in the Web-Mercator projection so the lake geometry is honest. Colour-code stations by any bottle variable. This is ODV's Station Map.

📉 Bottle Profiles

A bottle-chemistry variable plotted against depth (deep = bottom), the classic profile. Compare several stations at once to see how the surface bloom, nutrients, or chemistry change with depth.

🌡️ CTD Casts

High-resolution sensor profiles vs depth — temperature, salinity, oxygen and more — for the stations the CTD was deployed at. This is what a continuous cast looks like next to the discrete bottles.

🛰️ Live

Stream and log real-time data from the YSI Bluetooth/GPS sonde — live readouts, a rolling chart, and a moving track on the map. A built-in Demo mode simulates a cast so you can see how it works offline. Logged readings flow into the other tabs as the Live sonde dataset.

🔬 Property–Property

Any variable against any other, optionally colour-coded by a third — exactly ODV's X/Y scatter. Great for spotting relationships (e.g. nutrients vs. chlorophyll, or ion ratios that fingerprint water sources).

🌊 Section

ODV's signature view: stations laid out south→north along the cruise track, depth on the vertical axis, and a variable shown as a smooth colour field. We interpolate between samples (inverse-distance weighting) to mimic ODV's gridded sections — handy, but remember the colour between dots is a guess.

📋 Data Table

The cleaned dataset, sortable and searchable, with a one-click CSV download.

💬 Talk to the Lake

An online AI assistant that answers questions about this data and its connection to Lake Winnipeg. It's the only tab that needs an internet connection.

A few honest notes on the data

  • Values like N.D. (not detected) and blanks are treated as missing, not zero — they're left out of plots and stats.
  • The original file lost its delta symbols, so the isotope columns are shown here as δ¹⁵N and δ¹³C (‰).
  • Two pH columns (pH [1], pH [2]) are replicate measurements; many cells are blank.
  • CTD station names were tidied (e.g. STN 59 → S59); above-water readings (depth ≤ 0) and unphysical negative PAR values are dropped.
  • The satellite basemap was pre-fetched once (Esri World Imagery) and embedded, so the map needs no internet.
  • This is raw teaching data — part of the exercise is judging which numbers look trustworthy. Always sanity-check before drawing conclusions.

💬 Talk to the Lake

Ask questions about this dataset and how it relates to Lake Winnipeg — e.g. "Why is chlorophyll so high at S2 but low just below the surface?" or "What do the Ca/Na and Mg/Na ratios suggest about where the water comes from?" You'll need an internet connection, and answers can take up to a minute.

⚠️ The assistant can be wrong — treat it as a study buddy, not a citation. It sees the full bottle dataset and a per-station summary of the CTD casts (not every raw row). Always cross-check its answers against the plots and the data table.
